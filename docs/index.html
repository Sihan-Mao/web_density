Urban geometry refers to the contiguous built-up area expanding outward from city centers.


Click on city centroids or example cities' names to zoom in. Then, use the slider below to observe how a city’s urban geometry evolves each year.


Urban geometry is derived from the WSF Evolution dataset (2001–2015) using contour analysis. The calculation of geometric metrics follows Harari (2020) and Angel et al. (2010). Urban population and experienced density calculations follow Duranton and Puga (2020) using WorldPop data (2001–2018). Emissions data is sourced from Shan et al. (2018). Naïve density data is sourced from the China City Construction Yearbook (2001–2018).
